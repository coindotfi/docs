  ## Overview

  CoinFi AAMM revolutionizes cross-chain trading by integrating quantum-secure cryptography, zero-knowledge proofs, and dynamic automated market maker (AMM) pricing. Our platform addresses inefficiencies in traditional atomic swaps, such as slippage, rigid execution conditions, and counterparty risks, offering a scalable, trustless, and cost-effective solution for decentralized asset trading.

  - **Quantum Security:** Utilizes zkHPNG for prime number generation, Fullmen for tamper-evident commitments, and RWP for proof verification to ensure transactions are secure against quantum computing threats.
  - **High-Frequency Trading:** Processes orders in 5-second rounds, categorizing trades into large and small orders for optimized matching and reduced slippage.
  - **Flexible Exit Mechanism:** Unmatched orders enter an exit queue, allowing multiple retry attempts before forced bridging, minimizing collateral usage and transaction costs.
  - **Economic Benefits:** Reduces bridging fees, enhances liquidity efficiency, and increases returns for liquidity providers, with an estimated annual ROI of 8-12%.

  ## How It Works

  <div class="section">
    <h2>Mathematical Foundation</h2>
    <ul>
      <li>
        <strong>Constant Product Formula:</strong>
        <div class="math">x * y = k</div>
        where <span class="math">x</span> and <span class="math">y</span> are token amounts in the liquidity pool, and <span class="math">k</span> is a constant that maintains pool balance, ensuring fair pricing.
      </li>
      <li>
        <strong>Bridging Efficiency:</strong>
        <div class="math">alpha_bridge = (1 - eta)(1 - eta_e)^nu</div>
        <ul>
          <li><span class="math">eta</span>: Efficiency of direct matches (approximately 95%).</li>
          <li><span class="math">eta_e</span>: Efficiency during exit queue rounds.</li>
          <li><span class="math">nu</span>: Number of exit rounds (default 3) before forced bridging.</li>
        </ul>
        This formula reduces bridging fees by up to 95% compared to immediate bridging, minimizing collateral usage.
      </li>
      <li>
        <strong>Groth16 Verification:</strong>
        <div class="math">e(a, b) = e([alpha beta], [T]) * e(Prod[A, b], [gamma]) * e(c, [delta])</div>
        Ensures zero-knowledge proof validity for transaction integrity.
      </li>
      <li><strong>Holy Prime Condition:</strong> Requires both <span class="math">P</span> and <span class="math">2P + 1</span> to be prime for quantum resistance.</li>
      <li><strong>Merkle Root:</strong> Aggregates commitments as <span class="math">C_r = MerkleRoot(C_1, ..., C_n)</span>, ensuring tamper-evidence.</li>
      <li>
        <strong>Exit-Queue Collateral:</strong>
        <div class="math">L_bridge = max(0, Sum{o in U_left} u(o) - Delta)</div>
        Calculates leftover volume for bridging, optimizing liquidity.
      </li>
    </ul>

    <h2>Core Axioms</h2>
    <ol>
      <li><strong>Atomic Execution:</strong> Every swap either executes fully or reverts completely.</li>
      <li><strong>Zero-Knowledge Soundness:</strong> Groth16 zk-SNARK proofs ensure only valid witnesses produce verifiable proofs.</li>
      <li><strong>Quantum Resistance:</strong> Primes are accepted only if both <span class="math">P</span> and <span class="math">2P + 1</span> are prime.</li>
      <li><strong>Merkle-Based Commitments:</strong> Fullmen commitments <span class="math">C = H(P concat E concat pk)</span> are aggregated into a tamper-evident Merkle root.</li>
      <li><strong>Recursive Proof Aggregation (RWP):</strong> Individual proofs are linked recursively for secure aggregation.</li>
      <li><strong>Exit-Queue Consistency:</strong> Leftover volume <span class="math">l</span> at round <span class="math">R_n</span> either bridges immediately with fee <span class="math">phi</span> or enters an exit queue for up to <span class="math">nu</span> rounds, preventing indefinite stalling.</li>
    </ol>
