## Summary of Equations Used

This section summarizes the key equations used in the QOTP and ENIGMA systems, aligning with the cryptographic principles outlined.

1. **Universal Language:**  
<div className="equation">
  $\Lambda = \lbrace c_1, c_2, \ldots, c_{40} \rbrace$
</div>
*Explanation:* Defines the 40-character alphabet used for both QOTP and ENIGMA, including A–Z, 0–9, and special symbols (♦, ♣, ♥, ♠).

2. **Private Key:**  
<div className="equation">
  $\Omega = (\Omega_1, \Omega_2, \ldots, \Omega_n), \quad \Omega_i \in \Lambda$
</div>
*Explanation:* Represents the private key as an ordered sequence of subkeys, each drawn from the universal language $\Lambda$.

3. **Secret Synonym Mapping (Holographic Morphism):**  
<div className="equation">
  $\Psi: \Gamma_i \rightarrow \Xi_i$
</div>
*Explanation:* Maps hyperplanes ($\Gamma_i$) to bearing directions ($\Xi_i$), forming a private, static mapping known only to the observer.

4. **Commitment:**  
<div className="equation">
  $\Gamma = H(\Omega \parallel H(\Psi))$
</div>
*Explanation:* Combines the private key $\Omega$ and the hashed mapping $H(\Psi)$ using a quantum-resistant hash function $H$ (e.g., SHAKE256), with `\parallel` denoting concatenation.

5. **$\Sigma$-Interactive Protocol Steps (Per Round):**  
<div className="equation">
  $\begin{aligned}
    & \text{Commitment:} \quad \text{Comm}_i = H(\Omega_i \parallel \Xi_i \parallel r_i) \\
    & \text{Response:} \quad s_i = f(\Omega_i, \Xi_i, e_i, r_i) \\
    & \text{Verification:} \quad \Theta(\text{Comm}_i, s_i, e_i)
  \end{aligned}$
</div>
*Explanation:*  
  - **Commitment:** Hashes the subkey $\Omega_i$, witness $\Xi_i$, and nonce $r_i$ using $H$.  
  - **Response:** Computes $s_i$ based on $\Omega_i$, $\Xi_i$, challenge $e_i$, and $r_i$.  
  - **Verification:** Uses $\Theta$ to verify the commitment, response, and challenge, ensuring zero-knowledge properties.

6. **Witness Sequence:**  
<div className="equation">
  $\Xi = (\Xi_1, \Xi_2, \ldots, \Xi_n)$
</div>
*Explanation:* Represents the sequence of witnesses (bearing directions) corresponding to each subkey, derived from the hyperplane mapping.
