## Overview

This document details our **quantum–resistant authentication system**. It explains:

- **Public Keys:** The only way to identify a wallet owner.
- **Private Keys:** The only way an owner can sign and send messages.
- **Quantum One–Time Pad (QOTP):** A dynamic, color–coded pad that only the owner can solve.

## Quantum One–Time Pad (QOTP) Legend

Our QOTP is now a **40–character sequence** that cycles through four colors. The full domain consists of:

- **A–Z** (26 characters)
- **0–9** (10 characters)
- **♦, ♣, ♥, ♠** (4 characters)

Each character’s color is determined by its zero–indexed position modulo 4:

- **Index mod 4 = 0:** **Red** → **UP**
- **Index mod 4 = 1:** **Green** → **DOWN**
- **Index mod 4 = 2:** **Blue** → **LEFT**
- **Index mod 4 = 3:** **Yellow** → **RIGHT**

The pad sequence is:

## Example: Möbius One–Time Pad Rotation

We simulate a Möbius rotation on our one–time pad by cyclically shifting the ordering. In each round, the pad is divided into four "color cards" (displayed as a 2×2 grid), with characters assigned based on their zero–indexed positions modulo 4.

> **Legend Recap:**
> - **Red** → **UP**
> - **Green** → **DOWN**
> - **Blue** → **LEFT**
> - **Yellow** → **RIGHT**

Below are the rounds:

### Round 1 (No Rotation)

**Original pad grouping:**

- **Red** (indices 0, 4, 8, …, 36):  
  **A, E, I, M, Q, U, Y, 2, 6, ♦**

  **B, F, J, N, R, V, Z, 3, 7, ♣**
- **Blue** (indices 2, 6, 10, …, 38):  
  **C, G, K, O, S, W, 0, 4, 8, ♥**
- **Yellow** (indices 3, 7, 11, …, 39):  
  **D, H, L, P, T, X, 1, 5, 9, ♠**
