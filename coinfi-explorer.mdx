# ARPC  
#### *Authentication Remote Procedural Call*

Traditional <abbr title="Remote Procedure Call: a protocol enabling a computer to request a service from a program on another computer without needing to understand network details.">RPC</abbr> protocols only enable basic client-server calls. ARPC, however, adds a robust authentication layer that cryptographically verifies the caller's identity before any action is taken. This strong verification is the foundation of Notary.

Notary is a dedicated blockchain explorer—a vigilant guardian in the digital realm. It continuously monitors and records phishing attempts, scam traps, and malicious contract deployments, strengthening the trustless infrastructure of decentralized systems. By integrating Notary with the CoinAuth SDK, any wallet can execute transactions with quantum-resistant security. Every transaction signing attempt—successful or not—is permanently recorded across supported blockchain networks. If an unauthorized use of a <abbr title="Private Key: a secret number that grants access to your cryptocurrency funds.">private key</abbr> is detected, the rightful owner is immediately alerted via our secure messaging system, ensuring an unbreakable chain of accountability.

# Securing Digital Identities: A New Vision for Crypto Security

Decentralization is more than a buzzword—it's a vision for true digital empowerment. Inspired by [Satoshi Nakamoto](https://bitcoin.org/bitcoin.pdf), we must address threats that undermine our digital ecosystem. Phishing, scam traps, and leaked private keys are not just technical glitches; they fracture the very trust that decentralized systems rely on. Our mission is to improve security practices and restore confidence in the digital tools that empower us.

> *Satoshi Nakamoto: "What is needed is an electronic payment system based on cryptographic proof instead of trust, allowing any two willing parties to transact directly with each other without the need for a trusted third party."*


## Understanding the Threats

Modern phishing attacks employ deepfakes and advanced algorithms to impersonate trusted entities. These scams trick users into relinquishing control of their digital assets. At the same time, honeypot scams lure victims into traps that compromise their funds. Meanwhile, private key breaches reveal the weaknesses in centralized systems.

Each threat challenges our goal of a secure, trustless environment where every individual maintains complete control over their assets.

## Shortcomings of Today's Systems

Many current wallet solutions and blockchain explorers provide only a partial view of network activity. Without a comprehensive monitoring system, unusual activities may go unnoticed until it's too late. This fragmented approach creates gaps that attackers can exploit.

True decentralization requires tools that let every user independently verify transactions and secure their assets.

## The Path Forward

Imagine a tool that records every transaction with cryptographic certainty and flags irregularities in real time. Such a system would integrate wallet data seamlessly, alert users to potential intrusions immediately, and offer complete transparency. This isn't a distant dream—it's the next evolution in digital security.

Our solution is simple yet powerful: a decentralized record-keeping mechanism that safeguards private keys while providing users with real-time insights. This is the future of wallet security—where protection and transparency go hand in hand.

## Glossary

- **RPC**: Remote Procedure Call – a protocol that allows one computer to request a service from another without needing to understand the underlying network details.
- **Private Key**: A secret number that provides access to your cryptocurrency funds.
- **Quantum-Resistant**: Security measures designed to protect against attacks from quantum computers.
- **Deepfakes**: AI-generated synthetic media that can convincingly replace a person's likeness.
- **Honeypot Scam**: A deceptive trap designed to lure users into losing funds.
- **Zero-Knowledge Proof**: A method to prove that a statement is true without revealing any additional information.
- **CoinAuth SDK**: A toolkit that enables secure transaction authentication in crypto wallets.
